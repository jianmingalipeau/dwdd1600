Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
First signer of the Declaration of Independence. Known for his large, bold signature symbolizing defiance toward the British Crown. Served as President of the Second Continental Congress.
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration of Independence. Became the 3rd President of the United States and was one of America’s most influential political thinkers.
Benjamin Franklin
Prominent scientist, inventor, diplomat, and statesman. Instrumental in securing French support during the Revolutionary War and a key figure of the American Enlightenment.
John Adams
Strong advocate for independence in the Continental Congress. Became the 2nd President of the United States and helped negotiate the Treaty of Paris ending the Revolutionary War.
Samuel Adams
Leader of the Sons of Liberty and key organizer of the Boston Tea Party. Influential political voice pushing for American independence.
George Wythe
America’s first law professor and mentor to Thomas Jefferson. Highly respected legal mind and early advocate against slavery.
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the resolution declaring that the colonies “are, and of right ought to be, free and independent states.”
Roger Sherman
Only person to sign the Declaration of Independence, the Articles of Confederation, and the U.S. Constitution.
Charles Carroll
Only Catholic signer of the Declaration and the longest-lived signer, dying in 1832.
Button Gwinnett
Signer from Georgia whose signature is extremely rare and sought-after by collectors.

Each of these individuals played a significant role in the founding of the United States and is remembered for their courage in signing the Declaration, an act considered treasonous by the British Crown.
